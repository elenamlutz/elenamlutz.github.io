Strong men; thoughtful women

Three out of five comic book characters have at least one superpower, regardless of gender. When we analyze the data for enhanced physical ability, we find that men in this category have more skills than women in comic books. Women are only known for being agile, whereas men have strength, stamina, super eating and other physical characteristics.

When we take an in-depth look at other abilities, we find that men have less thought-induced powers. Thor has his hammer, Iron Man has his suit, and other male characters are insanely rich or have other objects that enable their superpowers. On the contrary, women have less physical demanding powers like empathy, telepathy, and emotional control, which shows a clear stereotype for both genders.
